Kyoto

Kyoto is famous for its classic Buddhist temples, gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses, the city is also celebrated for its formal traditions such as kaiseki, a multi-course haute cuisine, and geisha, the traditional female entertainers in the Gion district.

Top 5 Attraction

  1. Kinkaku-ji

    Zen Buddhist temple that is literally covered in gold leaf, reflecting brilliantly in the pond it overlooks

    Kinkaku-ji Official Website

  2. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

    Walk through the towering bamboo grove Arashiyama, which is especially beautiful when there is a light wind and the tall bamboo stalks sway gently back and forth.

    Arashiyama Tourism Website

  3. Philosopher's Path

    A stone path following a canal lined by hundreds of cherry trees, named after the famous Japanese philosopher Nishida Kitaro who was said to meditate while walking

  4. Nijo Castle

    A flatland castle in Kyoto, Nijo Castle is noted for its beautiful gardens and architecture that reflects the opulence of the Edo period.

Travel Tips

  • Purchase a Kyoto City Bus & Subway Pass for unlimited travel on buses and subways within the city.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as you'll be walking a lot, especially when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Stay in a traditional Ryokan for at least one night to experience Japanese hospitality.
  • Try local Kyoto cuisine, like kaiseki and matcha sweets, for authentic flavors.
  • Visit Kyoto during the cherry blossom season (late March to early April) or the autumn leaves season (November) for the most picturesque scenery.
  • Respect local customs: speak softly, remove shoes when entering homes and certain temples, and bow slightly when greeting.
  • Use the 'ICOCA' rechargeable smart card for convenient tap-and-go payment on public transport.

Exlpore More
